A new & improved athletic clothes shopping process

A personal project for a customizable shopping crate for athletic clothes

The Problem:

When buyers shop for performance athletic clothes, they are given many similar product options. Buyers are frustrated by the difficulty in comparing, trying on, and selecting the right performance clothes. How might we simplify the process of selecting the products that best meet their needs?

The Solution:

A build-your-own shipment of athletic clothes that recommends options for the user based on their requirements. Your pre-selected clothes will be shipped to your house, and the user will be able to try on clothes for fit, comfort and style. After comparing items, the user will keep what they wish to purchase and send back what they do not.

The Process:

I initially approached the user problem with an idea: “Purchasing athletic clothes is a difficult process.” I confirmed this through user research in the form of interviews, where I discovered that the problem was extensive: users were exhausted by the process of comparing and purchasing athletic clothes that would meet their needs. The process was lengthy, often starting with internet browsing and ending with in-person shopping at many similar athletic clothes to ensure fit and comfort of competing styles. Using design-thinking, I was confident that I would create an easy streamlined process that would solve the pain points of my users while keeping their needs and goals in mind.

Identifying Pain Points in competitor processes:

I began with user interviews to better understand what the pain points, needs and goals were for my users. I synthesized this information through affinity mapping, which helped me to fully understand the journey of my users. I also examined the buying process of athletic retailers with a competitive analysis to compare user pain points to the online and in-person buying processes that they had been navigating through.

Developing a Persona:

Based off the interviews and competitive research I incorporated user needs, motivations and concerns/frustrations to create a representative user of my product. By taking the time to create a persona, I had an easy way to refer back to my end user throughout the design process. Having a persona helped to create empathy and ensured that I was considering my user in every step of the journey.

Mapping out the user experience:

Ideating through storyboards helped me to understand what the ideal user journey should be. I started with very abstract and impossible solutions (transportation, and personal shopper) to help me dial back and realize realistic problems to solve the user’s pain points and streamline their process (a crate of personalized athletic clothes shipped to your door).

I also incorporated user flows to help me map out the user experience. One of the main frustrations of my persona was the difficult and lengthy process of comparing and finally purchasing the right product. My solution had to be streamlined and easy to use -- user flows helped me to map out the user’s journey and ensure that it met the needs and goals of my user while also solving their frustrations.

Prototyping, usability testing and iteration:

With my user flows in mind, I designed paper prototypes for my initial design, as they are an inexpensive and quick way to test and iterate on design ideas. I assigned 5 different users a series of tasks such as “create a crate to compare athletic tops” and “compare and try on a few different Nike cleats” to see how real users were able to use the product.

Through my initial round of testing I was able to iterate and improve the product before applying visual design. From there, I transitioned into Sketch to create a low fidelity prototype that went through an additional round of testing.

Finally, I was ready to add color and typography to my design in the form of a high fidelity prototype. I put this prototype through a final round of usability testing to ensure that my persona’s needs and goals were met, and that her frustrations were resolved by my solution.
